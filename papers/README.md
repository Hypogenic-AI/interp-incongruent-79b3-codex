# Downloaded Papers

1. [The Linear Representation Hypothesis and the Geometry of Large Language Models](park_2023_linear_representation_hypothesis_llms.pdf)
   - Authors: Kiho Park, Yo Joong Choe, Victor Veitch
   - Year: 2023/2024
   - Source: arXiv / ICML 2024
   - URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.03658
   - Why relevant: Core formalization of LRH; defines unembedding/embedding representations and the causal inner product used to reason about concept geometry.

2. [The Geometry of Categorical and Hierarchical Concepts in Large Language Models](park_2024_geometry_categorical_hierarchical_concepts_llms.pdf)
   - Authors: Kiho Park, Yo Joong Choe, Yibo Jiang, Victor Veitch
   - Year: 2024/2025
   - Source: arXiv / ICLR 2025
   - URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.01506
   - Why relevant: Extends LRH from binary contrasts to features, categorical concepts, and hierarchies; most directly relevant to incongruent versus separable concept structure.

3. [Toward a Flexible Framework for Linear Representation Hypothesis Using Maximum Likelihood Estimation](nguyen_2025_flexible_framework_lrh_mle.pdf)
   - Authors: Trung Nguyen, Yan Leng
   - Year: 2025
   - Source: arXiv
   - URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.16385
   - Why relevant: Extends LRH beyond single-token counterfactual pairs with an activation-difference estimator (SAND), useful for harder or ambiguous concepts.

4. [The Geometry of Concepts: Sparse Autoencoder Feature Structure](li_2024_geometry_of_concepts_sae_feature_structure.pdf)
   - Authors: Yuxiao Li, Eric J. Michaud, David D. Baek, Joshua Engels, Xiaoqing Sun, Max Tegmark
   - Year: 2024/2025
   - Source: arXiv / Entropy 2025
   - URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.19750
   - Why relevant: Provides adjacent evidence that learned concept spaces exhibit linear analogy structure, clusters, and global geometry beyond pairwise probing.

5. [On Linear Interpolation in the Latent Space of Deep Generative Models](michelis_2021_linear_interpolation_latent_space_dgms.pdf)
   - Authors: Mike Yan Michelis, Quentin Becker
   - Year: 2021
   - Source: OpenReview / ICLR Workshop
   - URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2105.03663
   - Why relevant: Sharp statement that straight-line interpolation can be arbitrarily misleading relative to geodesics; anchors the information-geometry side of the project.

6. [Feature-Based Interpolation and Geodesics in the Latent Spaces of Generative Models](struski_2019_feature_based_interpolation_geodesics.pdf)
   - Authors: Lukasz Struski, Michal Sadowski, Tomasz Danel, Jacek Tabor, Igor T. Podolak
   - Year: 2019
   - Source: arXiv
   - URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/1904.03445
   - Why relevant: General interpolation framework for non-Gaussian latent densities and feature-constrained interpolation.

7. [The Riemannian Geometry of Deep Generative Models](shao_2017_riemannian_geometry_deep_generative_models.pdf)
   - Authors: Hang Shao, Abhishek Kumar, P. Thomas Fletcher
   - Year: 2017
   - Source: arXiv
   - URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/1711.08014
   - Why relevant: Foundational paper on pull-back metrics, geodesics, and parallel transport in learned latent manifolds.

8. [Should Semantic Vector Composition be Explicit? Can it be Linear?](cohen_2021_semantic_vector_composition_linear.pdf)
   - Authors: Dominic Widdows, Kristen Howell, Trevor Cohen
   - Year: 2021
   - Source: ACL Workshop on Semantic Spaces
   - URL: https://aclanthology.org/2021.semspace-1.8/
   - Why relevant: Positions the linear-versus-nonlinear compositionality question in semantic vector spaces and helps frame the incongruent-composition motivation.
