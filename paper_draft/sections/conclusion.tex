\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We studied interpolation for incongruent text concepts with a paired, path-centric evaluation. A control experiment on curated LRH assets showed that \embmodel still supports strong linear contrasts in separable settings. The main experiment then showed that incongruence does not produce a universal interpolation penalty. At the pooled level, incongruent paths were slightly shorter and less off-manifold than congruent paraphrase controls. The decisive result is subset-specific: relation replacements increase off-manifold deviation and dual-space path length, while object and attribute swaps often do not because they remain lexically close to the source.

The key takeaway is that interpolation failure tracks structural incompatibility more than contradiction alone. Future work should test the same question in hidden-state spaces with explicit LRH estimators, use benchmarks whose negative endpoints are not systematically closer than paraphrases, and adopt stronger manifold estimators so geodesic detour becomes informative instead of degenerate.
